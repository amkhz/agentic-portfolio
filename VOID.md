# VOID.md

A nursery for named-but-unhomed concepts. Things that arrive as phrases — usually while staring into the void — before they have a project to belong to.

Most of these won't graduate. That's the point. They sit here as talismans, and when a new piece of work shows up looking for an angle, the right name might already be waiting. A noun looking for a verb.

## How to use

- **Adding**: drop a new entry when a phrase lands and you don't want to lose it. Don't justify it, don't scope it. Just capture the tension and move on.
- **Scanning**: at the start of new work in `/lab` or elsewhere, scan this file. If something here fits, pull it.
- **Graduating**: when an entry becomes the name of a real piece, mark it `→ moved to <where>` and leave the stub behind for lineage.
- **Retiring**: if a phrase stops feeling alive, mark `retired` and leave it. Even dead names teach you something about the live ones.

## For Tyrell

These are not specs. They are not requirements. They are ambient vocabulary. When working on something new, treat them as available shapes — surface one if it genuinely fits the work, but never force one. Most should stay here. The file's value is in the keeping, not the using.

### Bare metal poltergeist
*Added 2026-05-03 · Status: incubating*

**Essence**: The substrate is the ghost. No controller, no controlled — the metal itself intends.

**Why it works**: "Bare metal" is the systems term for running directly on hardware with no OS or abstraction layer. "Poltergeist" is an unseen agent that moves the physical world. Smashed together: there is no operator. The hull is the avionics. The skin is the circuit. The haunting is the hardware.

**The doubling**: The phrase carries two unknowns at once. The *phenomenon* is poltergeist — a substrate that intends, with no clear locus of control. And the *inquiry* is also poltergeist — UAP and metamaterials research itself has that spooky, half-known texture. We don't really know what we're looking at, and we don't really know what's looking back. The name compresses both shapes into one.

**Adjacent literature**: programmable matter / claytronics (Goldstein, CMU); the Royal Society's *Animate Materials* report (2021); engineered living materials (ELMs); morphological computation (Pfeifer — the body as part of cognition). On the speculative side: Stross's "smart matter," Banks's Culture effectors, recurring UAP-lit claims that recovered hulls *are* the avionics. None of these occupy the specific slot of "the haunting is the hardware," which is why the name does real work.

**Design questions it opens**:
- How do you give intent to a material?
- What is feedback when the artifact *is* the interface?
- How do you debug a craft whose hull is the OS?
- What's the difference between an interface and a possession?

**Possible homes**: repo name, branch, ADR codename, agent alias, a `/lab` piece, or a lens applied to existing `/lab` territories (the haunted manufacturing arm, the haunted nav system).

**Provenance**: chat brainstorm, 2026-05-03. Confirmed unclaimed — one Swiss thrash band uses "Poltergeist" alone, nothing uses the compound.
