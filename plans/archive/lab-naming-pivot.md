# Feature: Lab naming pivot

## Summary

Rename the lab currently shipping as **Frontier Lab** at `labs.justinh.design` to **Perihelion**. The surface is now a two-arm house: **Perihelion Archive** (research / comprehension / teaching) and **Perihelion Works** (applied design: systems, interface, human factors, product). Target register was astronomical, alchemical, poetic/spatial, open, honest, inspiring, tongue-in-cheek sci-fi, with a decisive invitational principle (the lab exists to inspire other designers who have not yet considered frontier science).

## Decision (locked 2026-04-21)

| Role | Name |
|---|---|
| Brand | **Perihelion** |
| Research arm | **Perihelion Archive** |
| Design-practice arm | **Perihelion Works** |

**Rationale in one paragraph.** Perihelion (the point in an orbit closest to the sun, the moment of maximum attention) is astronomical, precise, and invitational rather than gatekept. It reads as a brand standalone, rewards the reader who learns what it means, and ages well across many volumes. Archive signals curated, preserved, worth keeping, which matches the actual shape of the deep-dive content better than "library" would, and avoids circularity with the pre-rename "research library" framing. Works carries motorsport factory-team energy (manufacturers racing their own craft) that reads equally well as "selected works" for designers and "public works" for systems thinkers. The two anchors sit as peers, both craft-coded, both single-noun, both dual-duty.

## Context

"Frontier Lab" is too close to a trope in the speculative-design and VC adjacent ecosystem. Near Future Laboratory, Frontier Tower, a dozen "labs," and the word "frontier" itself are doing heavy lifting for too many brands. Justin wants a word that stands out the way the portfolio's own aesthetic does: warm, honest, a little sideways.

This is a re-brand of a shipped surface, not a new launch. Eight guides are live. ADR-009 governs the architecture and subdomain. The rename affects identity copy and chrome only. Guide content, architecture, tokens, and deploy topology are all untouched by this pivot.

### What the name must do

1. **Carry identity as a single word.** Someone should be able to say "I was reading something on [name] last night" and have it track.
2. **Support an anchor word without needing one.** "Perihelion Press," "Perihelion Review," or just "Perihelion" should all read as the same brand.
3. **Feel designed, not trendy.** No SaaS pattern ("Signal," "Orbit," "Launchpad"). No startup cliché ("Forge," "Foundry"). No frontier-adjacent tropes ("Horizon," "Beacon," "Summit").
4. **Reward the reader who digs into it.** The name should have a precise meaning in its original discipline that also functions as metaphor for what the lab does. Perihelion (closest approach to the sun) is the calibration example.
5. **Age well over years of growing content.** The name is a container for many volumes. It should not feel tied to any one guide's topic.
6. **Sit next to the portfolio, not copy it.** Portfolio is "Blade Runner + William Gibson meets Finn Juhl." The lab is a sibling with its own scholarly-adjacent register. Warm, serious, quietly witty.

### What the name must not do

- Require an accent mark or a non-English character to parse at a glance.
- Share a name with a well-known existing brand, film, or band that would swamp search results.
- Sound clinical, corporate, or administrative ("Research Group," "Institute," "Advisory").
- Be a pun so loud that it becomes the whole point.

## Naming brief

### Positioning

- **What it is:** a growing library of deep-dive research guides on frontier science and speculative design territories.
- **Who reads it:** researchers, practitioners, curious technologists, the UAP science community, design peers.
- **Posture:** reader's notebook. Prep, not product. Learning first, applied design second.
- **Tone:** honest, specific, self-aware. Willing to joke about itself. Willing to take a paper very seriously.

### Register shortlist

Justin gave five vectors. Every candidate below sits in one or more of them.

| Vector | What it looks like |
|---|---|
| Astronomical | Precise celestial terms repurposed as brand words |
| Alchemical | Laboratory vessels, transformations, pre-scientific craft |
| Poetic / spatial | Place-words that evoke state or vantage |
| Open, honest, inspiring | Humble-aspirational, fit for "prep not product" |
| Tongue-in-cheek sci-fi | Winks at genre without cosplay |

### Primary-word criteria

- 2 to 5 syllables, with a rhythm that carries aloud
- Greek or Latin etymology acceptable, even preferred
- Obscure enough to feel like a find, specific enough to mean something
- Available as a brand, domain-wise and search-wise
- Works naked or with an anchor

### Anchor-word palette

Ordered roughly from most publishing-coded to most architectural:

- **Press** — small press, editorial energy
- **Review** — editorial authority, periodical
- **Almanac** — annual reference, temporal rhythm
- **Atlas** — reference map, navigational
- **Library** — literal, reading-room
- **Notes** — humble, diary-ish, close to voice
- **Notebook** — already in the tagline
- **Observatory** — astronomical echo, watch-and-record
- **Study** — academic, architectural
- **Field** — broad discipline, exploratory
- **Compendium** — book of many things

## Shortlist (historical)

Eight finalists from the deliberation round. **Perihelion selected.** Retained here as decision context for ADR-010.

### 1. Perihelion

**Rationale:** Justin's tuning signal. Closest approach to the sun is the exact moment of maximum attention, which is what a deep-dive guide engineers. Astronomical, precise, poetic, not in widespread brand use.

**Anchor recommendations:**
- **Perihelion Press** (top): small-press publishing energy. Serious without being stuffy. Reads as a brand on first encounter.
- Perihelion Review: editorial authority. A little more journal, a little less playful.
- Perihelion Observatory: astronomical echo. Risks becoming self-parody.
- Perihelion alone: works in spoken reference. The anchor stays optional.

**Conflict flag:** Common astronomy term. Some niche products exist (video games, albums, a biotech). "Perihelion Press" is clean on a quick search. Worth a real clearance pass before lock-in.

### 2. The Penumbra

**Rationale:** The partial shadow zone during an eclipse. Liminal, evocative, under-claimed. "The" as the only anchor gives the word ceremonial weight. Matches the academic-preprint visual register already shipped.

**Anchor recommendations:**
- **The Penumbra** (top): definite article carries it.
- Penumbra Press: works, a bit heavier.
- Penumbra Notes: humble, softer.

**Conflict flag:** Penumbra is also a horror video game series and a common legal term. Medium search collision. "The Penumbra" as a publication brand is cleaner.

### 3. Parallax Notes

**Rationale:** Parallax is the apparent shift of a thing when viewed from two angles. That is the literal operation of design research: look twice, from different positions, and the real shape appears. Pairing with "Notes" grounds the astronomical precision with a humble anchor that matches the voice profile.

**Anchor recommendations:**
- **Parallax Notes** (top): designy front, humble back. Matches the reader's-notebook positioning exactly.
- The Parallax Atlas: aspirational, more book-like.
- Parallax Press: clean but a little generic.

**Conflict flag:** Parallax Press exists as a real publisher (Berkeley, Buddhist and social justice titles). That likely kills "Parallax Press" as a direct name. "Parallax Notes" should be clear.

### 4. Slow Light

**Rationale:** Real physics phenomenon (light slowed in a medium to walking speed, observed experimentally). Also direct commentary on slow reading versus feed reading. Two-word elegance. Probably the most designy option on the list.

**Anchor recommendations:**
- **Slow Light** (top): no anchor. The two words ARE the brand.
- Slow Light Press: works, slightly long.

**Conflict flag:** "Slow light" appears in physics literature and occasional art projects. No dominant brand on it. Low conflict.

### 5. Syzygy Almanac

**Rationale:** Syzygy is the alignment of three celestial bodies. Rare, rhythm-heavy, immediately memorable. Pairing with "Almanac" (a book of measured events indexed by time) is extremely tongue-in-cheek sci-fi in the best way. The name nearly teaches itself.

**Anchor recommendations:**
- **Syzygy Almanac** (top): the pairing is the point. Alignment-of-bodies plus reference-of-events.
- Syzygy Press: funkier, shorter.
- Syzygy alone: strong standalone for spoken reference.

**Conflict flag:** Syzygy is used in academic journals and a few small bands. Low conflict, high memorability.

### 6. The Alembic

**Rationale:** The alchemical still. A vessel where raw material is distilled into something refined. That is what a reading-notebook does to a paper. Beautiful sound, old-world craft association, fits the warm-blacks-and-brass aesthetic.

**Anchor recommendations:**
- **The Alembic** (top): definite article anchors it.
- Alembic Press: works, a bit heavy.
- Alembic Notes: humble, softer.

**Conflict flag:** Alembic.ai is an AI company. The Alembic is also a jazz venue name in some cities. Medium search collision but limited brand collision with a publication.

### 7. The Cairn

**Rationale:** A stone pile marking a path, left by travelers who came before for travelers who follow. That metaphor is almost too on the nose for a reader's-notebook library. Humble, open, honest, inspiring. Matches Justin's voice profile ("prep, not product") almost exactly.

**Anchor recommendations:**
- **The Cairn** (top): standalone with article.
- Cairn Press: clean.
- Cairn Notes: a bit redundant (cairns ARE notes of a kind).

**Conflict flag:** Cairn is a common outdoor brand name (Cairn Energy, Cairn the subscription box). Medium conflict. "The Cairn" as a publication is cleaner but still search-collides.

### 8. Aphelion Press

**Rationale:** Aphelion is the farthest point from the sun in an orbit. The opposite of Perihelion. If Perihelion is the moment of closest attention, Aphelion is the long thinking from a distance. Could be a stronger fit for the lab's slow-reading posture than Perihelion, paradoxically.

**Anchor recommendations:**
- **Aphelion Press** (top): publishing energy.
- Aphelion Review: editorial.
- Aphelion alone: works.

**Conflict flag:** Aphelion is less commonly used than Perihelion in branding. Likely cleaner for clearance.

## Decision criteria

In order of weight:

1. **Spoken test.** Say the name aloud in a sentence. Does it carry?
2. **First-encounter test.** A new visitor sees only the header. Do they understand this is a publication, not a product?
3. **Metaphor test.** When the reader learns what the word means, does it make the lab feel more like what it already is?
4. **Availability test.** A five-minute search: is there a dominant brand on this word that would muddy attribution?
5. **Voice test.** Read the voice profile's sample lines with the new name inserted where "Frontier Lab" lives. Does it still sound like Justin's voice?
6. **Longevity test.** Imagine forty guides. Does the name still fit?
