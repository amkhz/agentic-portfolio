Overall

The deck's motion has a real grammar and it is obeyed: springs never overshoot, the authored moments bloom once and hold, and the two lanes stay in their lanes. What remains is finish work — two spots animate layout where transform belongs, and two reactive exits are missing or over-long. Nothing here breaks the doctrine.

Where the timings land

The deck spends its duration budget deliberately: the boot ritual and commit ceremony are authored narrative moments and earn their deliberate-zone dots. The reactive lane mostly sits where it should — the outliers are one over-long caption relay and two interactions with no transition at all.

Deliberate · 300–500ms — earned by the authored ceremonies: boot beats, the commit's tighten and handoff.
Sluggish · 500ms+ — narrative pacing only. Fine for the chamber and the settle; wrong for a caption that answers a drag.
What's off

Dots 7 and 8 are authored reading-pace moments and earn their zone. Dot 6 is the mismatch: a reactive caption riding mode="wait" with a full-length spring exit, so a boundary crossing waits up to a second for its words. And the two hollow dots — the draft wipe and the commit hover — are reactive interactions with no transition at all.

ImportantJakub

Drafts spring in, but vanish instantly on redraft

WhatProposal cards enter on the arrival spring, and the commit path exits them beautifully via the authored timeline. But the other exit path — the operator changes intent and DeckSession passes [] while drafting — unmounts all three cards in one frame. No AnimatePresence, no exit.

Why it mattersIt is the classic half-built-motion tell: a surface that glides in and blinks out. On the deck it also breaks the fiction — drafts that were "on the bench" should be withdrawn, not deleted. This is the piece's primary decision surface.

Recommended motionWrap the cards in AnimatePresence with an exit subtler and quicker than the enter: opacity: 0, y: -3 over ~160ms ease-in. Guard the commit path (cards are already at opacity 0 when the timeline finishes, so the extra exit is invisible, but skipping exit while committing keeps ownership clean).

ImportantJakubEmil

The regime caption's exit is as heavy as its enter — and the drag waits for it

WhatThe slider's regime line swaps through AnimatePresence mode="wait" with deckSpringSoft (perceptual ~500ms) on both enter and exit, and a symmetric ±3px travel. A boundary crossing therefore pays up to a full second before the new words sit still.

Why it mattersThis caption answers the operator's hand mid-drag — it is reactive-lane copy, not an authored beat. Exits should be subtler than enters, and a wait-mode swap serializes the two. The crossing pulse acknowledges the boundary in 320ms; the words shouldn't lag the light by triple.

Recommended motionKeep the spring on the enter. Make the exit a quick tween — opacity: 0, y: -2 at ~150ms ease-in (transition.exit override). The relay reads as one gesture: old line releases, new line lands on the spring.

What's working well
  • ✓Every spring is at or past critical damping: deckSpringSoft declares bounce: 0; the thumb (ζ ≈ 1.29) and the needle (ζ ≈ 1.33) are overdamped. No arrival overshoots anywhere. deckMotion.ts:10-21, ParadigmSlider.tsx:42

  • ✓Reduced motion is architectural: prefers-reduced-motion routes the whole session to the static plate, with a live matchMedia listener, plus a CSS guard on the one looping animation. deck-mode.ts:23, useDeckCapabilities.ts:39, flight-deck.css:1288-1293
  • ✓ease: "none" where the value is a readout — the hold scrub and the substance-transfer handoff are linear because they are the playhead and the conserved quantity. Honest, not lazy. bootTimeline.ts:60-71, commitTimeline.ts:47-55
  • ✓No animation on keyboard-driven state that shouldn't have it: the range input is native, and the spring that follows it is the piece's core mechanic (the dissolve must ride a continuous value), which is the sanctioned exception.
Issues to address
ImportantEmil

The slider thumb tracks by animating `left` — layout work on every frame of a drag

WhatThe spring's live value is transformed into a percentage string and applied as left on the absolutely-positioned thumb. Every spring frame during a drag invalidates layout — on the same frames the dissolve writes opacity to five regions and the WebGL field renders.

Why it mattersThis is the deck's single hottest interaction. Layout properties can't be composited; transform can. The visual result is identical, the frame budget is not — and the deck already pays a per-frame tax for the shader and the dissolve. Cheapest headroom available.

Recommended motionMeasure the track once (resize-observed) and drive x in pixels: useTransform(spring, v => v * trackWidth) on a thumb whose CSS keeps only the static centering offset (e.g. margin-left: -50%-of-thumb instead of the translate). Same spring, same feel, zero layout.

ImportantEmil

The certification sweep animates `left` from 0% to 100%

WhatEach non-canvas instrument's boot sweep tweens left: "0%" → "100%" on the absolutely-positioned 1px arm — 850ms of per-frame layout, repeated per instrument during the ritual, exactly while GSAP is also flashing lamps and raising captions.

Why it mattersSame class of cost as the thumb, in the deck's showpiece sequence. The boot is where a dropped frame is most visible — it is the one moment the operator is watching the motion itself. Boot-feel tuning is an open thread; free frames first.

Recommended motionKeep left: 0 static and tween x to the measured region width: tl.fromTo(el, { x: 0 }, { x: () => body.clientWidth, ... }) — GSAP resolves function-based values at play time, so the layout-effect ordering is unchanged. Same linear arm, compositor-only.

What's working well

  • ✓Hold-to-start as a scrubbed playhead: the hold drags the timeline forward, an early release runs it backward at a faster drain rate. The gesture and the motion are the same object. bootTimeline.ts:59-71, DeckSession.tsx:418-448
  • ✓The substance-transfer handoff — the route trace is consumed at exactly the rate the field's arrival glow rises. Narrative physics instead of a crossfade, and it carries the piece's whole thesis. commitTimeline.ts:46-55
  • ✓The one pulsing element in the entire deck is the dormant breath lamp — the invitation, reduced-motion-guarded, and extinguished by the first touch of the playhead. A pulse with a job. flight-deck.css:1271-1293, bootTimeline.ts:59
  • ✓Judgment already applied where it counts: the boot settle's positional overshoot was cut in live review because it read as a bounce — the breath now lives in brightness and the traveling bloom. bootTimeline.ts:176-183

No issues found at this severity level.

Opportunities
  • 💡The alert row extinguishes instantly when the drill moves on — world-honest for an annunciator, but a ~150ms opacity release timed with the lamp's existing releaseAlertLamp tween would let the light and the words leave together. AlertRegion.tsx:25-38, drillTimelines.ts:89-95
